A FEW TIPS TO GET YOU STARTED
------------------------------

Use simple, meaningful titles that credit the original source. These titles will appear in the playlist.

Classification
Classifications are broad categories that describe the type of audio. Pro-tip: use as few as applicable.

  Ambient        — drone, ambient music, longer static audio, minimalist music
  Atmospheric    — field recordings, found sounds, city ambiance, crowd noise
  Environmental  — natural sounds: thunder, bird calls, rainfall, ocean waves
  Premixed       — already assembled mixes: radio excerpts, mixed music tracks
  Soundscape     — complex audio environments, theatrical sound design
  Archival       — historical recordings: old radio, speeches, old TV shows
  Spoken Word    — talks, poetry readings, speeches, interviews
  Narrative      — radio dramas, storytelling, narrative podcasts
  Instructional  — language lessons, how-to guides, training programs
  Vocal Music    — music featuring vocals
  Instrumental   — classical, instrumental jazz, ambient instrumental
  Experimental   — noise music, sound experiments, unconventional audio
  Digital        — synthesizer music, electronic beats, computer-generated
  Effect         — short clips for layering: sound effects, stingers, transitions
  Other          — unique or rare audio that doesn't fit elsewhere

Tags
Tags describe the topic, theme, or texture — things like "rainy night", "jazz", or "whispering". Use as many simple tags as you can think of, separated by commas. Rather than compound tags like "1950s-educational-film", use simple ones like "1950s, educational, instructional". The system handles plurals automatically.

A word about copyright
We think copyright is too often a way of locking in corporate profits, but we do want to show respect for other artists. Good sources include recordings in the public domain (archive.org), Creative Commons (freemusicarchive.org), public performances captured by spectators, and recordings so obscure that the crime of using them is offset by the opportunity that someone actually gets to hear them.
